# Community Cuts for Kids — First Activation Production Brief

Status: working production brief
Event: Community Cuts for Kids / Back-to-School Bash
Date: Sunday, August 30, 2026
Time: 12:00 PM–3:00 PM
Location: Tangles & Locs, 7425 Hardeson Rd, Everett, WA 98203
Campaign: Supplies for Success, July 15–August 15, 2026

## Strategic decision

Treat the first event as a controlled pilot, not a mass-attendance campaign.

The goal is not maximum reach. The goal is a well-run first public activation that:

- serves the number of families Asc3nd can realistically support;
- protects youth and family dignity;
- avoids overpromising haircuts, supplies, food, or capacity;
- creates a reliable RSVP and follow-up list;
- generates authentic founder and community story assets;
- produces measurable evidence for the next event, sponsors, donors, volunteers, and the main Asc3nd website.

Until operating capacity is confirmed, distribution should stay primarily within Asc3nd's warm network, existing partners, participating businesses, families, churches, schools, coaches, mentors, and direct community relationships. Do not use paid promotion or broad boosted distribution before capacity is known.

## Critical facts to establish in today's interview

These answers control the RSVP cap and public promises:

1. How many barbers/stylists are confirmed?
2. Roughly how many haircuts can each complete per hour?
3. Are cuts first-come-first-served, appointment-based, or RSVP-based?
4. What ages or eligibility rules apply?
5. How many backpacks/supply kits or individual supplies are realistically available?
6. Is every attendee expected to receive supplies, or while supplies last?
7. What food is confirmed and approximately how many people can be served?
8. How many volunteers/staff are confirmed for check-in, lines, supplies, food, photography/consent, cleanup, and family questions?
9. What is the safe physical occupancy of the venue/event footprint?
10. Who is the public event contact?
11. Who should receive new RSVP notifications?
12. What partners/sponsors may be named publicly today?
13. What physical supply drop-off locations are confirmed?
14. Is there an approved online donation method?

Do not publish assumptions as facts.

## Capacity rule

Before broad distribution, calculate a practical service capacity from the smallest limiting resource:

- haircut capacity;
- supply capacity;
- food capacity;
- venue capacity;
- staffing/check-in capacity.

Use an operating buffer rather than booking to the theoretical maximum.

The public RSVP system should support:

- RECEIVED — RSVP submitted, details received;
- CONFIRMED — participation confirmed under the current capacity rules;
- WAITLISTED — current capacity reached;
- CANCELLED;
- ATTENDED;
- NO_SHOW.

Until capacity is verified, the automatic email should acknowledge receipt without promising a haircut, supply kit, food item, or guaranteed service.

Capture Otha and Elisha:

## Core interview questions

Ask naturally. Follow good answers instead of rushing through the list.

### Origin

1. Why did you create Asc3nd?
2. Was there a specific moment when you realized this organization needed to exist?

4. What does the name Asc3nd mean to you personally?

### The young person and family

5. What do you want a young person to feel when they encounter Asc3nd?
6. What do you think young people need from adults and community that is often missing?
7. What do you want parents and guardians to know about how Asc3nd approaches youth?

### Community Cuts for Kids

8. Why start with a back-to-school event?
9. Why do haircuts and school supplies matter beyond the objects themselves?
10. What would make this first event feel successful to you, even if it is not a huge event?
11. What do you want a family to feel when they leave?

### Beyond one day

12. What happens after Community Cuts for Kids?
13. How do mentorship, leadership, life skills, athletics, and community connection fit together?
14. What kind of relationship do you want Asc3nd to build with families over time?
15. What role can mentors, volunteers, businesses, churches, schools, and community partners play?

### Future and invitation

16. If Asc3nd is working exactly as you hope three years from now, what is different for young people and families?
17. What do you need from the community right now?
18. What would you say directly to a parent deciding whether to bring their family to this event?
19. What would you say directly to a potential mentor or volunteer?
20. Finish this sentence in your own words: "Asc3nd exists because..."

# Controlled promotion plan

## Principle

Do not optimize for maximum impressions.

Optimize for the right number of expected attendees relative to real capacity.

## Phase A — warm network first

Distribute the redesigned digital flyers through:

- founders' direct contacts;
- Tangles & Locs customers/community;
- confirmed partners;
- churches;
- coaches/mentors;
- local family/community relationships;
- direct text/email/DM;
- printed hand-to-hand flyer distribution where appropriate.

Every flyer routes to the standalone event landing page with a QR code.

Use source-tagged URLs where practical, such as:

- `?src=founder`
- `?src=tangles`
- `?src=partner`
- `?src=print`
- `?src=social`

Store the source with the RSVP.

## Phase B — expand only if capacity supports it

After RSVP pace and real resources are known:

- broader organic social;
- partner reposts;
- additional community distribution.

Do not use paid/boosted promotion for the first pilot unless capacity and operations clearly support it and the owner explicitly approves the spend.

# RSVP system requirements

## Public form — minimum useful fields

Required:

- adult contact name;
- email;
- estimated group size;
- consent to event-related contact.

Optional:

- phone;
- number of children in the group who may want a haircut, if operationally required;
- structured interest selection: attend / volunteer / donate supplies / sponsor / partner.

Do not collect:

- child names;
- child ages unless a later operational/legal requirement is explicitly approved;
- youth stories;
- medical/mental-health/family details;
- unrestricted free-text notes.

## Persistence

Persist server-side to the approved Asc3nd data store/Supabase boundary.

Suggested RSVP record:

- id
- event_slug
- adult_name
- email
- phone_optional
- group_size
- haircuts_requested_optional
- interest
- source
- contact_consent
- status
- created_at
- updated_at
- idempotency_key

Use unique/idempotent handling to reduce duplicate submissions.

Do not expose service-role credentials to the browser.

## Email workflow

On successful server-side persistence:

1. send attendee receipt/confirmation email;
2. send staff notification or create a staff task;
3. record email delivery attempt/status;
4. preserve an audit event.

Until capacity is verified, attendee wording should say the RSVP was received and that final participation/service details will follow. Do not promise a haircut, supplies, food, or guaranteed service before capacity rules are confirmed.

Later reminders may be sent only after contact consent and event operations are confirmed.

# First-event measurement

Track only useful evidence.

## Before event

- landing-page visits;
- RSVP submissions;
- RSVP source;
- confirmed attendees;
- waitlist if used;
- volunteer interest;
- mentor interest;
- supply donors;
- sponsor/partner leads.

## Event

- actual attendees;
- youth/families served using non-sensitive aggregate counts;
- haircuts completed;
- supplies distributed;
- volunteers participating;
- partners participating;
- major operational bottlenecks;
- consented story/photo/video assets captured.

## After

- follow-ups completed;
- new volunteers/mentors;
- partner conversations;
- donor/sponsor opportunities;
- approved testimonials;
- reusable photo/video assets;
- lessons for the next event.

Success is not raw crowd size. Success is a safe, dignified, well-run event that creates trust, evidence, relationships, and a repeatable operating model.
