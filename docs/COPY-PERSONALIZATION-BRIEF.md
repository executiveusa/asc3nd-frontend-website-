# Copy Personalization Brief — Asc3nd Public Site

## Current copy problem

The current homepage explains the operating system. That is useful for selling Mission OS, but the live Asc3nd frontend should primarily explain Asc3nd Collective: who it serves, what outcomes it creates, why Seattle should trust it, and how people can help.

## Public site structure to build next

1. Hero
   - clear mission headline;
   - youth/sports/mentorship focus;
   - primary CTA: volunteer, donate/sponsor, partner, or refer youth;
   - secondary CTA: see impact.

2. Who We Serve
   - youth;
   - families;
   - schools;
   - coaches;
   - mentors;
   - community partners.

3. Programs
   - sports mentorship;
   - leadership development;
   - wellness and life skills;
   - school/community partnerships;
   - sponsor-supported opportunities.

4. Outcomes
   - attendance;
   - mentorship hours;
   - youth served;
   - volunteer hours;
   - events;
   - scholarships/sponsor support;
   - stories.

5. Get Involved
   - volunteer;
   - sponsor;
   - partner;
   - donate;
   - refer a youth/family;
   - request a call.

6. Trust + Safety
   - youth safeguarding;
   - approval-first AI operations;
   - human review;
   - privacy posture;
   - board/fiscal/501c3 status as available.

7. AI-readable resources
   - `/llms.txt`;
   - structured data;
   - grant-ready mission page;
   - impact page;
   - program pages;
   - FAQ.

## Sample replacement hero copy

Headline:

> Helping Seattle youth build confidence, discipline, and opportunity through sports, mentorship, and community support.

Lead:

> Asc3nd Collective connects young people with coaches, mentors, sponsors, and safe programs that turn local support into measurable progress.

Primary CTAs:

- Volunteer or mentor
- Sponsor a youth opportunity
- Partner with us
- Refer a family

## Copy rule

The public site should lead with mission. The admin/backend can explain the operating system.
